The Plain Tiger's Life Cycle

The final stage of the Plain Tiger butterfly's life cycle is the adult stage. After completing its transformation within the chrysalis, the adult butterfly emerges with wings that are brownish orange, with black and white markings . The adult butterfly has a wingspan of about 75 mm and a body length of approximately 23 mm . The adult stage can last for 10-15 days for males and 7-12 days for females . During this stage, the butterfly focuses on reproduction, flying from flower to flower to obtain nectar and mate.

    Once the larva reaches its final instar, it enters the pupa stage. The larva becomes motionless and ceases feeding, changing color from gray to brown . This prepupal stage lasts for 1-3 days . The larva then forms a chrysalis, a hard, protective casing, and undergoes a period of transformation within it . The pupal stage lasts for 9-15 days, depending on the temperature . During this time, the pupa changes color from pale green to dark brown.

The first instar is small, about 4 mm long, with a white body and black head . As the larva progresses through the instars, it grows larger and develops distinct yellow and black stripes . The larval stage can last from 12 to 20 days, depending on the temperature . During this stage, the caterpillar feeds on various plants, primarily from the Asclepiadoideae family, including milkweed.
